Investigate and understand why the three interior angles of any triangle always add up to 180° (two right angles).

Key ideas

  • For any triangle, ∠A + ∠B + ∠C = 180°.
  • One common proof: draw a line parallel to one side through the opposite vertex → use alternate/corresponding angles and the fact that a straight line equals 180°.
  • This theorem holds for all triangles in Euclidean geometry (acute, right-angled, obtuse).

Quick check

  • In a triangle the angles are 50° and 60° — find the third angle.
  • Explain why a triangle can’t have angles 90°, 60°, 40° (sum is too large).
  • Sketch a triangle, draw a line parallel to one side, label alternate angles and show how the sum emerges.
