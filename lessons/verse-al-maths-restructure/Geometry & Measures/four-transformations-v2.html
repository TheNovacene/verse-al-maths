2. The Human Story: Who Was Emmy Noether?

"To trace the paths of lives before, and ask what justice calls us for."

Emmy Noether (1882–1935) was a groundbreaking German mathematician. Despite being barred from formal teaching roles because of her gender, she made critical contributions to abstract algebra and theoretical physics. Noether’s Theorem connected symmetries in nature with laws of conservation — a bridge between maths and the physical world.

“The guiding principle in my work has always been to find what remains invariant.” — Emmy Noether

Justice Prompt: Why is it important to learn about mathematicians like Emmy Noether? How do her struggles and achievements relate to gender equality in education and careers today?

4. The Designed World: Symmetry and Structure in Society

Consider how symmetry appears in art, architecture, music, and nature. Symmetry creates balance — a principle that Emmy Noether proved is also mathematical.

Explore:

  • Take a photo of something symmetrical in your environment.
  • Design a logo or pattern using transformations (e.g. reflect and rotate shapes).
  • Research how symmetry and algebra are used in encryption, coding, or game design.
5. The Living Body

Movement Prompt: Try this: mirror a friend’s movement exactly — hand gestures, posture, steps. Then reverse roles. How did it feel to reflect? What did you notice stayed the same or changed?

Wellbeing Prompt: When do you feel most balanced or symmetrical in your thinking or routine?
