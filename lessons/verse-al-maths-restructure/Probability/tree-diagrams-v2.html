Focus Concept: Using Tree Diagrams to Represent Independent and Conditional Probability

Tree diagrams help us map all possible outcomes of multi-stage events and calculate the probabilities of combinations.

Key Ideas:

  • Independent events: The outcome of one stage does not affect the next (e.g., flipping two coins).
  • Conditional events: The outcome of one stage influences the probabilities in the next stage (e.g., drawing counters without replacement).

Use this interactive tool to build your own tree diagrams and explore how probabilities multiply along each branch.
