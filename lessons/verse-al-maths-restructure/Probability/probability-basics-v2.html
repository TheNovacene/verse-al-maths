Focus Concept: Theoretical vs Experimental Probability

The theoretical probability of an event is what we expect to happen in a perfectly fair system. For example, the chance of rolling a 7 as the sum of two dice is 6 out of 36, or 1/6.

The experimental probability is what we actually observe when we do an experiment — like our horse race!
