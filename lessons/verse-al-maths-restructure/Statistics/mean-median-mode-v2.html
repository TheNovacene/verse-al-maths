Seed of Change (KS3 Statistics): Use and interpret measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode) to describe and compare data sets.

Copy of Mean, Median, and Mode Data Lab Web App for Students by Gerry Docherty

Key Ideas:

  • Mean – add all the numbers and divide by how many there are.
  • Median – put the numbers in order and choose the middle value (or the mean of the two middle values).
  • Mode – the value that occurs most often (there can be more than one mode, or no mode).

Try this using the Data Lab above:

  1. Enter a small data set (for example: 3, 5, 5, 7, 9).
  2. Notice what the app shows for mean, median and mode.
  3. Change one of the numbers. Which measures change? Which stay the same?

Your Task: Create a short “data story” using mean, median and mode.

Choose one option:

  1. Option A – Class or Fictional Data Set
    • Invent (or use real but anonymised) data for at least 10 people (e.g. number of messages in a day, hours of sleep, game scores).
    • Calculate the mean, median and mode.
    • Write a short explanation of which average tells the fairest story and why.
  2. Option B – Scenario Critic
    • Choose one scenario from Activity 3, or make your own.
    • Explain which average is most honest and which could be misleading.
    • Suggest how you would present the data to keep it fair.

What to Submit (for Canvas Assignment):

  • A data set (list of numbers) and your calculated mean, median and mode.
  • 1–2 paragraphs explaining which average you chose to highlight and why.
  • Optional: a screenshot or sketch from one of the interactives that influenced your thinking.

Assessed using the Haven/GCSE Maths Rubric:
