In this lesson, you’ll explore what happens when an exponential function is reflected in an axis:

  • Reflection in the x-axis: \( y = -a^x \)
  • Reflection in the y-axis: \( y = a^{-x} \)

  • Exponential function: A function like \( y = a^x \), where \( a > 0 \)

“How does the world reflect us — and how do we reflect the world?”

Mathematics uses reflection to model equality and balance. In design, architecture, and even music, reflections offer symmetry and beauty. In social systems, they remind us of fairness, justice, and mirroring another’s experience.
