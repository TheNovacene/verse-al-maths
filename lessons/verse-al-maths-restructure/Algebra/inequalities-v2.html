üß† The Knowing World

Inequalities show ranges of values that are true, not just one answer.

Key Concepts

  • Inequality symbols: <, ≤, >, ≥
  • They compare values and define sets of possible solutions.
  • Solving inequalities is like solving equations – but be careful when multiplying/dividing by negatives!
  • Graphical representation on a number line helps visualise the solution range.

Try this

  • Solve and represent: \(x + 3 < 7\)
  • What happens when you solve: \(-2x \geq 6\)?
  • Can you write a compound inequality for: "Between 2 and 5, not including 2"?
