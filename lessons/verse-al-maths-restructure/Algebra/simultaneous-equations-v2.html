Simultaneous Equations

GCSE Maths | Pearson Edexcel (Higher) | 40–60 mins | Inquiry-Based | Neurodivergent-Friendly

üß† The Knowing World

What values of x and y satisfy both equations at once?

Key Concepts

  • Simultaneous equations are a pair of equations with two unknowns, usually x and y.
  • Substitution involves expressing one variable in terms of the other and substituting into the second equation.
  • Elimination involves adding or subtracting equations to eliminate one variable, then solving for the other.
  • The solution is the point where both lines would intersect on a graph.

Quick Check

  • Solve by elimination: \( \begin{cases} 2x + y = 10 \\ x - y = 1 \end{cases} \)
  • Solve by substitution: \( y = 3x - 2 \), \( 2x + y = 13 \)
  • What does it mean if the equations have no solution or infinite solutions?
üåç The Human Story

Simultaneous equations model real-world problems: budgeting, intersecting paths, comparing deals, or balancing forces. When two conditions must be true at once, simultaneous equations offer a solution space.
