Algebraic Fractions

GCSE Maths | Higher Tier | Inquiry-Based | 45–60 mins

üß† The Knowing World

Algebraic fractions are like ordinary fractions, but they include expressions with variables. We use the same rules to simplify, add, subtract, multiply, divide, and solve — with extra care for factoring and cancelling!

üìå Key Concepts

  • To simplify: Factor both numerator and denominator, then cancel common terms.
  • To add/subtract: Use a common denominator — just like numeric fractions.
  • To multiply: Multiply numerators together, and denominators together — then simplify.
  • To divide: Multiply by the reciprocal of the second fraction.
  • To solve: Clear denominators (if needed) and solve the resulting equation.

üîç Quick Check

  • Simplify: \( \frac{x^2 - 1}{x - 1} \)
  • Add: \( \frac{2x + 3}{6} + \frac{x - 1}{3} \)
  • Multiply: \( \frac{x + 2}{x - 1} \cdot \frac{x - 1}{x + 4} \)
  • Solve: \( \frac{3x}{x + 2} = 6 \)
üåç The Human Story

Fractions date back to ancient Egypt — where they wrote them all as sums of unit fractions. Algebraic fractions emerged much later, helping mathematicians model real-world problems like speed, rates, and scaling. In modern life, algebraic fractions are used in engineering, computing, and financial modelling.
