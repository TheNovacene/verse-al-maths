Creating & Solving Formulae from Word Problems

GCSE Maths | Pearson Edexcel (Foundation & Higher) | 40–60 mins | Neurodivergent-Friendly

üß† The Knowing World

Mathematics often begins with a story. Turning a story into an equation is like translating a new language — the language of algebra.

Key Concepts

  • Identify the unknown and represent it with a letter.
  • Turn relationships into mathematical expressions.
  • Build and solve equations to find unknown values.

Examples

  • “A number plus 7 equals 15” becomes: \( x + 7 = 15 \)
  • “A rectangle has area 30cm². Its length is twice its width” becomes: \( x(2x) = 30 \)
üåç The Human Story

Formulae are everywhere — from budgeting at home to tracking fitness and designing buildings. They help us organise information and make decisions. Humans invented algebra to solve real problems, from measuring fields to building pyramids.
