Factorising Non-Monic Quadratics

GCSE Maths | Pearson Edexcel (Higher) | 40–60 mins | Inquiry-Based | Neurodivergent-Friendly

üß† The Knowing World

When can a quadratic expression like \( ax^2 + bx + c \) be rewritten as a product of two brackets?

Key ideas

  • When \( a \ne 1 \), we can use a method called "splitting the middle term".
  • Multiply \( a \times c \) and find two numbers that multiply to that and add to \( b \).
  • Rewrite \( bx \) as two terms using those numbers, then factor by grouping.
  • Check by expanding to ensure the original expression is recovered.

Quick Inquiry

  • Try factorising \( 2x^2 + 7x + 3 \)
  • Try \( 6x^2 + 11x + 3 \)
  • What do you notice about the numbers you need to find?
