Concept: Pythagoras’ Theorem can be extended to three dimensions by applying it in two perpendicular triangles — often involving paths through cuboids or pyramids.

In 3D, we often use the formula:

d = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2} — where d is the space diagonal in a cuboid, and x, y, z are its side lengths.

Prompt: What is different about a diagonal that passes through space compared to a diagonal on a flat surface?

üìò Glossary
  • Diagonal (3D): A line connecting opposite corners in space, not just on a face.
  • Cuboid: A 3D shape with 6 rectangular faces.
  • Perpendicular Triangles: Two right-angled triangles that share a side at right angles to each other, often used in multi-step Pythagoras problems.
