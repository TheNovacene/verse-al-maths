Surveyors, engineers, and architects rely on trigonometry to measure things without direct access. Knowing one angle and one side length, they use trig to determine heights, distances, and slopes. It’s essential in fields like civil engineering, aviation, and robotics.

üìö Glossary
  • Hypotenuse: The side opposite the right angle — always the longest.
  • Opposite: The side opposite the angle you’re using.
  • Adjacent: The side next to the angle you’re using (not the hypotenuse).
  • SOHCAHTOA: A memory trick for which sides go with sin, cos, and tan.
