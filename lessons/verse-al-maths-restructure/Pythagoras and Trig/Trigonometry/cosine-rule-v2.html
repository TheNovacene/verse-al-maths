üß† 1. The Knowing World

Concept: The Cosine Rule generalises Pythagoras' Theorem for any triangle and allows us to calculate missing sides or angles.

  • To find a side: a² = b² + c² - 2bc cos A
  • To find an angle: cos A = (b² + c² - a²) / 2bc

These formulae work in all triangles, not just right-angled ones.

üß¨ 2. The Human Story

Triangulation has long been used in navigation, astronomy, and surveying. The Cosine Rule helps calculate distances when angles and lengths are not easily measurable — for example, when mapping terrain or locating ships at sea.

Reflection: When have humans had to estimate distances without being able to measure directly?
