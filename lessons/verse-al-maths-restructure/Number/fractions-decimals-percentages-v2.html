Fractions, Decimals & Percentages — One Idea, Three Languages

GCSE Maths | Pearson Edexcel (Higher) | 40–60 mins | Neurodivergent-Friendly

üß† The Knowing World

FDP are three ways to express the same quantity. Today we’ll spot patterns and translate confidently between them.

Key ideas

  • Percent means “per 100”: 37% = 37/100 = 0.37.
  • Decimal → Fraction: place value gives the denominator (0.6 = 6/10 = 3/5).
  • Fraction → Decimal: divide numerator by denominator (1 ÷ 8 = 0.125).
  • Fraction → Percent: make denominator 100 or go via decimal (3/4 = 0.75 = 75%).
  • Benchmarks: 1/2 = 0.5 = 50%, 1/4 = 0.25 = 25%, 1/5 = 0.2 = 20%, 1/8 = 0.125 = 12.5%.
  • Recurring decimals: 1/3 = 0.3ÃÖ; 2/9 = 0.2ÃÖ.

Glossary (co-construct)

  • Terminating decimal — ends after a finite number of digits.
  • Recurring decimal — repeats a block of digits.
  • Equivalent — different forms, same value.
  • Percentage change — change ÷ original × 100%.

Quick check

  1. Write 0.45 as a fraction in simplest form.
  2. Convert 7/20 to a decimal and a percent.
  3. Which is larger: 0.27 or 1/4? Give a quick reason.
  4. Turn 62.5% into a fraction and a decimal.
