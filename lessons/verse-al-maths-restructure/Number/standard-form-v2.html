Standard form (scientific notation) lets us write very large or very small numbers neatly using powers of 10.

Key ideas & rules

  • A number in standard form looks like: \(a \times 10^n\), where \(1 \le |a| < 10\) and \(n\) is an integer.
  • To convert: move decimal point, count the places, use positive exponent for big numbers, negative for small numbers.
  • Multiplying & dividing in standard form: multiply/divide the \(a\) parts, add/subtract the exponents, then adjust to keep \(a\) between 1 and 10.
  • Addition/subtraction: you must make the exponents the same before combining the \(a\) parts.
  • Always express the final answer with \(1 \le |a| < 10\) — adjust and fix the exponent if needed.
  • Scientific calculator support: many calculators have an “EE” or “×10^” button for entering standard form directly.

Quick check

  1. Write \( 5{,}600{,}000 \) in standard form.
  2. Convert \( 0.000042 \) into standard form.
  3. Multiply \( 3.2 \times 10^5 \) by \( 4.5 \times 10^3 \).
  4. Add \( 7.5 \times 10^4 + 2.3 \times 10^3 \) (express result in standard form).
