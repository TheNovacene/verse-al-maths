ğŸ§¬ 2. The Human Story

Sophie Germain (1776–1831) was a self-taught French mathematician who overcame exclusion to contribute to number theory, elasticity, and physics. She studied primes during a time when women weren’t allowed in most universities — often using a male pseudonym to share her work.

She explored a unique category of primes now called Germain Primes, which play an important role in digital encryption and internet security today.

Justice Prompt (SDG 5): Why is it important to remember and share stories of mathematicians like Sophie Germain? What does gender equality in maths look like today?
