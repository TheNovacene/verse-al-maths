🧠 The Knowing World

“How do graphs shift left or right?”

In this lesson, we’ll explore what happens when a graph is translated horizontally by changing the input variable inside the function:

  • y = f(x + a) — the graph shifts left by a units
  • y = f(x - a) — the graph shifts right by a units

🧾 Glossary:

  • Translation: Moving a shape or graph without rotation or resizing
  • Input: The x-value of a function
  • f(x + a): Function shifted in the horizontal (x) direction
  • Horizontal shift: A movement of a graph left or right
🌍 The Human Story

“What does it mean to shift our path — to adjust our direction?”

Horizontal translations help model delays, phase shifts, and time changes. Whether scheduling a train, adjusting a sound wave, or modelling daylight hours, these shifts are part of life.

Guest Voice: Sound engineers and urban planners use horizontal shifts to adjust signals, design roadways, and plan accessible transport. Can you think of something in your world that was delayed — but still arrived?

🌱 SDG 11 – Sustainable Cities: When we understand how things move in time and space, we build smarter, fairer systems for everyone.

🌿 The Living Body

“Feel your weight shift gently left, gently right.”

  • Sit comfortably with both feet on the ground
  • Sway slightly to the left — then to the right — and notice the balance
  • Let your breath follow: in on the left, out on the right

Even graphs need room to shift. So do we.
