🧠 The Knowing World

“What happens when we lift or lower a graph?”

In this lesson, we’ll explore what happens when we shift a graph up or down by adding or subtracting a number to the function:

  • y = f(x) + a — the graph shifts up by a units
  • y = f(x) - a — the graph shifts down by a units

🧾 Glossary:

  • Translation: A shift in position (without changing shape)
  • Function: A rule that assigns each input (x) a single output (y)
  • f(x): Function notation, read as “f of x”
  • Vertical shift: Moving a graph up or down
🌍 The Human Story

“In a changing world, what must rise — and what must hold steady?”

Vertical shifts help us understand elevation, signal strength, prices, or population growth — concepts that change over time but follow predictable paths.

Guest Voice: Urban designers use vertical translations to model flood defenses, building heights, and transport ramps. What if we didn’t think mathematically about what needs to be raised?

🌿 SDG 11 – Sustainable Cities and Communities: Understanding vertical change helps us plan inclusive, accessible, and resilient environments.

🌿 The Living Body

“When graphs rise, we pause to breathe. When they fall, we ground again.”

Try this brief body check:

  • Sit tall. As you breathe in, imagine a graph rising.
  • As you breathe out, imagine it slowly lowering.
  • Repeat three times, visualising each shift as steady and calm.

💭 A graph’s movement mirrors our own — we all have highs and lows.
