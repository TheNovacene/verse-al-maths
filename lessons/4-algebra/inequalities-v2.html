Inequalities

GCSE Maths | Pearson Edexcel (Higher/Foundation) | 40–60 mins | Neurodivergent-Friendly

🧠 The Knowing World

Inequalities show ranges of values that are true, not just one answer.

Key Concepts

  • Inequality symbols: <, ≤, >, ≥
  • They compare values and define sets of possible solutions.
  • Solving inequalities is like solving equations – but be careful when multiplying/dividing by negatives!
  • Graphical representation on a number line helps visualise the solution range.

Try this

  • Solve and represent: \(x + 3 < 7\)
  • What happens when you solve: \(-2x \geq 6\)?
  • Can you write a compound inequality for: "Between 2 and 5, not including 2"?
🌍 The Human Story

Inequalities mirror real life – speed limits, price ranges, age restrictions. Not everything has one fixed value. How often do we make decisions within a range rather than a single number?

🎨 The Expressive Self
  • Create a poster showing how each inequality symbol works.
  • Use storytelling: write a short comic or skit where characters argue using inequalities.
  • Write an "Inequality Poem" exploring boundaries and limits.

🧘 The Living Body

Use arm gestures to show open and closed circles. Create a human number line with classmates – step forward if you’re part of the solution set!

✅ Final Task

Task: Create a worksheet or interactive slide with at least four inequalities from real life. Solve and represent them correctly.

  • Conceptual Understanding — Use inequality symbols correctly and explain their meaning.
  • Fluency — Solve one- and two-step inequalities accurately.
  • Application — Link inequalities to real-world scenarios.
  • Reflection — What do inequalities reveal that equations do not?
