Geometrical Constructions: Bisectors

GCSE Maths | Pearson Edexcel (Higher) | 40–60 mins | Inquiry-Based | Neurodivergent-Friendly

🧠 The Knowing World

Constructions in geometry use only a compass and straightedge—no measuring. We can create precise, logical diagrams using basic tools and ancient rules.

Key Concepts

  • Perpendicular Bisector: A line dividing another line into two equal parts at 90°.
  • Angle Bisector: A line that splits an angle into two equal angles.
  • These constructions rely on the properties of circles and symmetry.

Quick Check

  • Why do the arcs intersect above and below the line when creating a perpendicular bisector?
  • What do you notice about the point where two angle bisectors meet in a triangle?
🌍 The Human Story

Builders in ancient Egypt and Greece relied on geometric constructions to build straight roads, walls, and temples. Without digital tools, they used string, sticks, and chalk to achieve precision. Why do you think this method of construction has endured for thousands of years?

🎨 The Expressive Self
  • Draw your own geometric pattern using only a ruler and compass (or digital equivalent).
  • Write a creative description of a world where only constructed geometry is allowed.
🛠️ The Designed World

Use these interactive Web Sketchpad tools to explore classic constructions:

1️⃣ Constructing the Perpendicular Bisector of a Line

Use the compass to draw arcs above and below the line segment from each endpoint. Connect the intersections.

Open Perpendicular Bisector Sketch

2️⃣ Constructing the Angle Bisector

Place the compass on the angle’s vertex and draw an arc. Then use the intersection points to create overlapping arcs. Connect the vertex to where the arcs intersect.

Open Angle Bisector Sketch

Try This

  • Test what happens when you move the endpoints—does the bisector still behave the same?
  • Can you construct both bisectors in a triangle? What do you notice?
🧘 The Living Body

Hold both arms out to represent an angle. Use your other hand to “bisect” it by placing it directly in the middle. Try it with different angles formed by your limbs or friends!

✅ Final Task

Task: Create a poster or digital drawing that shows both constructions with clear steps and explanations.

  • Accuracy: Are your bisectors exact?
  • Explanation: Can you describe each step and why it works?
  • Creativity: Add your own artistic touch using only compass-based geometry.
