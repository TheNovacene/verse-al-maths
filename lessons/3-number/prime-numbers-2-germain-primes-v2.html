🔢 Germain Primes & Gender Equality

GCSE Maths | Higher Tier | Key Stage 4 | Neurodivergent-Friendly | 40 mins

Lesson Focus: Prime number exploration through the lens of gender justice

🧠 1. The Knowing World

📌 Key Terms:

  • Prime Number: A number greater than 1 with exactly two factors: 1 and itself
  • Germain Prime: A prime number p such that 2p + 1 is also prime

🔍 Try This:

  • Is 3 a Germain prime?
  • Try 7. What is 2p + 1? Is the result also prime?
  • What is the next Germain prime after 11?
🧬 2. The Human Story

Sophie Germain (1776–1831) was a self-taught French mathematician who overcame exclusion to contribute to number theory, elasticity, and physics. She studied primes during a time when women weren’t allowed in most universities — often using a male pseudonym to share her work.

She explored a unique category of primes now called Germain Primes, which play an important role in digital encryption and internet security today.

Justice Prompt (SDG 5): Why is it important to remember and share stories of mathematicians like Sophie Germain? What does gender equality in maths look like today?

🗣 3. The Expressive Self

Choose one:

  • Write a letter to Sophie Germain, telling her what you’re learning and asking her a question
  • Create a poster of “Famous Female Mathematicians” with quotes and key ideas
  • Make a number line collage showing all the Germain primes under 50
🏗 4. The Designed World

Use this interactive tool to test whether numbers are Germain Primes. Enter a number, check if it’s prime, calculate 2p + 1, and see if it’s prime too!

💡 Exploration Prompts:

  • What do you notice about the spacing between Germain primes?
  • Why are primes like these valuable in security systems?

🔐 Extension Task – Cryptography Connection

Modern encryption relies on large prime numbers to secure data. Because Germain primes ensure that both p and 2p + 1 are prime, they add strength to cryptographic keys.

🕵️‍♀️ Challenge: Create a “Secret Code” using a Germain prime pair (e.g. 11 and 23). Explain how the idea of primes helps keep information safe online.

🌀 5. The Living Body

💫 Sit comfortably and breathe deeply. Imagine the quiet focus Sophie Germain needed to work late at night, defying social expectations. What helps you to concentrate, even when things feel hard?

🎧 Try playing quiet instrumental music while you search for Germain primes or build your poster.

🛠 Final Task: Prime Discovery & Gender Justice

Choose One Project:

  • Create a “Germain Prime Gallery” showing the primes you tested, and what you found
  • Write or record a reflection about women in maths, and what fairness in education means to you
  • Design a “Maths + Justice” visual using quotes and facts from Sophie Germain’s life and your own learning

🎯 GCSE Maths Rubric Focus:

  • Conceptual Understanding – Accurately explains Germain primes and related calculations
  • Fluency & Accuracy – Correct prime-checking and ratio reasoning
  • Application to Problems – Uses primes in creative, contextual, or secure-system situations
  • Independence & Reflection – Demonstrates thoughtful connection to gender and identity in maths
