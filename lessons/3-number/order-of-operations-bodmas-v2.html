BODMAS — Order of Operations

GCSE Maths | Pearson Edexcel (Higher) | 30–45 mins | Neurodivergent-Friendly

🧠 The Knowing World

BODMAS is the rule that tells us which operations to do first in a mathematical expression. Without it, we would all get different answers!

Key rules

  • Brackets first
  • Orders (powers and roots)
  • Division and Multiplication (left to right)
  • Addition and Subtraction (left to right)

Examples

  • What is 5 + 4 × 3? → Multiplication first → 5 + 12 = 17
  • What is (5 + 4) × 3? → Brackets first → 9 × 3 = 27
  • What is 2 + 3²? → Powers before addition → 2 + 9 = 11
🌍 The Human Story

Imagine you're coding, doing science, or even splitting a bill. A single mistake in the order of operations can change the outcome completely. People developed rules like BODMAS to avoid confusion and ensure accuracy.
