🧠 The Knowing World

📌 Using Inverse Trigonometric Ratios to Find Missing Angles

Concept: Trigonometric ratios also allow us to work backwards — to find a missing angle when we know two sides of a right-angled triangle. This uses inverse functions:

  • sin⁻¹(opposite ÷ hypotenuse) = angle
  • cos⁻¹(adjacent ÷ hypotenuse) = angle
  • tan⁻¹(opposite ÷ adjacent) = angle

🌍 The Human Story

Surveyors and engineers often know two sides of a triangle — but need to find a missing angle to design slopes, ramps, or bridges. Calculating an angle without protractors is critical in the field.

🧘‍♀️ The Living Body

Pause after each calculation. Trig is intense — take a moment:

  • Stretch your arms open wide like triangle arms.
  • Look up toward the top angle and breathe in for 4, out for 6.
  • Sit upright like a triangle’s vertical side. Relax your hands.

📚 Glossary
  • Inverse Trig Function: The “undo” button for sine, cosine, and tangent — gives you the angle.
  • θ (theta): The symbol often used to represent an unknown angle.
  • Calculator Mode: Your calculator must be in degree mode to use sin⁻¹, cos⁻¹, or tan⁻¹ correctly.
  • Hypotenuse: Always the longest side, opposite the right angle.
