🌿 Tangent Graphs: Balance, Limits, and Wellbeing

GCSE Maths | Higher Tier | Key Stage 4 | 40 minutes | Interactive Online Session

🧠 1. The Knowing World

"To understand the laws that guide, the patterns hidden deep inside."

📘 Core Idea: What is a Tangent Graph?

The tangent function shows how the ratio tan(θ) = sin(θ)/cos(θ) grows rapidly near certain values of θ. Its graph includes asymptotes and a repeating curve with steep sections.

  • Period: π (it repeats every π radians)
  • Asymptotes: Where cos(θ) = 0 → vertical lines at ±π/2, ±3π/2...
  • Shape: Starts low, passes through origin, and rises steeply

🌀 Interactive Sketch: Tangent from the Unit Circle

Explore how the graph of y = tan(θ) is built from the unit circle. As the point rotates, its tangent (y/x) is traced and graphed dynamically.

Prompts for Exploration:

  • What happens to the tangent value near ±π/2?
  • Where does the graph pass through zero?
  • How are asymptotes and undefined values represented in the sketch?

📉 Graph Transformations and Wellbeing Thresholds

Explore how each part of the function y = a·tan(bx + c) + d affects the graph:

  • a – Vertical stretch or compression
  • b – Affects the period (wave length)
  • c – Horizontal shift
  • d – Moves the graph up/down

Interactive Model: Experiment with transformations below

Task: Adjust the parameters to design a graph that represents:

  • Heart rate under exercise vs. rest
  • Stress response to workload
  • Or a mental “overload” pattern
🌀 5. The Living Body

Breath Activity: Inhale slowly until your arms rise overhead. Exhale and lower. Repeat with calm awareness of rhythm.

🛠 Final Task: Modelling with Tangent – SDG 3

🌍 SDG 3: Good Health and Wellbeing
Design a tangent-based graph that models a real or metaphorical health pattern. Use it to reflect on body, balance, and boundaries.

Choose one focus:

  • Heart rate, stress, or blood pressure model
  • A metaphor for overthinking or energy spikes
  • Medication effect over time (rising then dropping off)

Your submission can include:

  • A graph of y = a·tan(bx + c) + d with labelled parameters
  • A paragraph or audio reflection connecting it to wellbeing
  • Optional: creative metaphor, drawing, or movement description
