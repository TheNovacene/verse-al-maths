🧠 The Knowing World

In this lesson, learners explore the use of trigonometric ratios in three dimensions. They revisit right-angled triangles from different perspectives and consider how these can appear within 3D shapes such as cuboids and triangular prisms.

Key concept: Trigonometric ratios still apply in 3D as long as the triangle in question is right-angled. Often, learners will be using two 2D triangles connected through a 3D shape to make deductions.

🌍 The Human Story

From climbing ramps to launching spacecraft, understanding angles in three dimensions helps us make sense of how objects move, rise, or tilt. Navigation, architecture, and engineering all use this kind of reasoning.

🛠️ The Designed World

Now it’s your turn to apply 3D trigonometry!

Use the Web Sketchpad interactive below to explore a ramp that extends from the base of a cuboid to its top far corner. By dragging a point, you change the height of the ramp and explore:

  • The angle of incline of the ramp
  • The angle of projection onto the base

Prompt: Adjust the ramp height, then calculate the two angles using right-angled triangle trigonometry. Can you generalise your approach for any rectangular prism?

🎨 The Expressive Self

Which part of 3D trigonometry felt most surprising? Did it remind you of a real-world object or journey?

  • Create a metaphor or image for how a 3D triangle sits inside a cuboid
  • Record an audio log describing what it feels like to “travel” along the ramp
🧘 The Living Body

Pause for a moment and stretch. Reach your arms up as if climbing a ramp. Feel the triangle formed between your arms, body, and the floor. How would you describe the angle?

Now take a breath and return to the sketch, refreshed.

✅ Final Task

Use the interactive sketch to calculate:

  1. The angle of incline of the space diagonal from base to top corner
  2. The angle of its projection onto the base

Present your work as:

  • A labelled diagram with calculations
  • A voice explanation or video demonstration
  • A slide deck walking through your reasoning
