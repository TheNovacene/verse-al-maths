📌 Exploring Sine, Cosine & Tangent Ratios

Welcome to your interactive trigonometry lesson! Dive into the relationships between sides and angles in right-angled triangles with these dynamic sketches.

In a right-angled triangle:

  • sin θ = opposite ÷ hypotenuse
  • cos θ = adjacent ÷ hypotenuse
  • tan θ = opposite ÷ adjacent

As you scale the triangle, the angles stay the same—and so do these ratios.

🌍 The Human Story

Trigonometry has long guided human exploration: from ancient sailors navigating oceans, to astronomers charting the skies, to modern architects planning structures.

Prompt: Imagine an ancient navigator using just triangles for voyages. What could she measure and why would it matter?

🎨 The Expressive Self

🛠️ The Designed World

The triangle ratios you explore can be used to design accessible ramps, control camera angles in games, or model sound waves in engineering.

Buddy Challenge: Design a ramp rising 0.5 m over 6 m horizontally. What angle does that ratio imply?

Ethical Design Question: How might you adapt your sketches or explanations to support someone with low vision or color-blindness?

🧘‍♀️ The Living Body

Take a moment to breathe deeply. As you breathe in, imagine drawing a triangle’s first side; hold, draw the next; breathe out, draw the third.

Reflect: Can you feel the ratio staying balanced even as the shape expands?
