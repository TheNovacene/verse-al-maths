🌿 Sine Graphs: Understanding Periodic Waves

🔢 Introduction to Radians

Before we graph sine waves, we need to understand radian measure — the natural way to measure angles in mathematics.

  • One full circle = 360° = 2π radians
  • π radians = 180° and π/2 radians = 90°
  • Radians are based on the arc length around a unit circle

Imagine wrapping a piece of string around a circle — the angle formed by the arc length is a radian!

🌀 Unit Circle Activity: Tracing the Sine Graph

This sketch lets you see how the sine graph is generated by tracing the y-coordinate of a point moving around a unit circle.

Prompts for Exploration:

  • What do you notice about the height of the point as it travels around the circle?
  • Where does the height reach its maximum and minimum?
  • How does the shape of the sine wave connect to the point’s vertical movement?

🌍 Real-World Relevance:
Sine waves appear in ocean tides, sound waves, and sonar used for underwater exploration. Understanding their patterns helps us map and protect marine life.

🎤 Guest Prompt:
What kinds of patterns might fish, divers, or whales experience below water?

✊ Justice Prompt:
Many communities rely on ocean tides for food and energy. How can understanding wave graphs support fairer access to resources?

🛠 Final Task: Mapping Ocean Waves

🌍 SDG 14: Life Below Water
Use sine graphs to model wave or sonar behaviour that supports sustainable marine research or energy generation.

Your task:

  1. Create a graph using y = a sin(bx) + k to model a wave.
  2. Label the amplitude, period, and vertical shift. Explain each part of your model.
  3. Describe how your model could be applied in a real-life context like sonar mapping or tidal research.
